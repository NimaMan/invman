A second, structurally different instance of this family corroborates the same lever. The
\emph{general-network divergent} (Kunnumkal--Topaloglu) instance is a one-supplier,
one-warehouse, three-retailer divergent tree carrying the same Kunnumkal--Topaloglu (2011) cost
structure as set~1 (warehouse holding $0.6$, retailer holding $1.0$, retailer backorder $19.0$,
no warehouse backorder) under unit lead times, with customer demand drawn as
$\mathrm{Poisson}(\alpha)$ whose per-period, per-retailer mean is itself resampled
$\alpha\sim\mathrm{Uniform}[5,15]$ each period --- a nonstationary mean. It is distinct both from the
CardBoard network above and from the divergent special-delivery (Gijsbrechts/Van-Roy) model treated
elsewhere in the paper.
Evaluating the published constant node-base-stock benchmark at levels $[124,30,30,30]$ in our
environment reproduces the published cost $4{,}059$ to within $-3.0\%$ (mean $\approx 3{,}931$, all
three retailer fill rates $\approx 98.6\%$), so this instance is treated as literature-verified, and
the learned soft tree --- warm-started at those same levels and trained under the identical
node-base-stock-targets design --- beats the reproduced benchmark by $\approx 37\%$ across two
independent optimizer seeds.
