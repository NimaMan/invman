Three further serial instances from \citet{snyder2019foundations} corroborate the same outcome across
more stages and both demand families: a $2$-stage system with $\mathrm{Normal}(100,15)$ demand, a
$5$-stage system with $\mathrm{Normal}(32,5.657)$ demand, and a $5$-stage system with
$\mathrm{Poisson}(32)$ demand (all with unit demand-facing lead time, installation holding decreasing
upstream, and a single customer backorder penalty; \texttt{serial/verification.rs}). Run under the
identical warm-started direct-level recipe, the learned soft tree \emph{reproduces} each instance's
proven Clark--Scarf optimum --- $166.2705 \to 166.2300$, $225.8672 \to 225.8047$, and
$226.8458 \to 226.8447$ respectively --- with every signed gap inside $0.03\%$ in magnitude, i.e.\
within the environment's own $\approx{\pm}0.06\%$ Monte-Carlo reproduction band and statistically
indistinguishable from the optimum (standard errors $\le 0.024$). As with Example~6.1 these are
\emph{matches}, not improvements: the comparator is a proven optimum and cannot be beaten.
