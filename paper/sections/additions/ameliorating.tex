\paragraph{Companion variants: the blending toggle and the capacity lever.}
Two further companion instances isolate single design levers under the same recipe and
protocol. A \emph{blending-enabled} variant (identical to the spirits anchor except per-period
blending is switched on, leaving the perfect-information bound unchanged at $1991.93$) gives a
clean ``value of blending'' contrast: the learned price-reactive purchase realizes the same
average profit as the no-blending anchor ($115.07\pm0.44$, again $\approx\!450\%$ and
$\approx\!140\times$ the paired SEM above the order-up-to gate), so on this instance enabling
blending does not move the single-purchase outcome---the issuance LP already draws the same aged
stock. A \emph{processing-capacity-constrained} variant (blending on, per-age capacity lowered
from $50$ to $30$, tightening the bound to $1663.89$) instead \emph{raises} learned average profit
to $130.49\pm0.50$ ($+109.58$, $+524\%$, $\approx\!157\times$ the paired SEM over the same gate):
the tighter capacity curbs holding on idle stock, and the gap to the (now lower) bound closes
slightly to $92.2\%$. In both variants the win is the like-for-like beat of the tuned order-up-to
gate; the LP value remains an upper bound, reported with its gap and never treated as achievable.
